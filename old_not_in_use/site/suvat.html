Physics Derivation Graph: SUVAT

  
 

For a derivation of SUVAT starting expressions, see the khanacadamy overview.

Starting from the wikipedia page on Constant translational acceleration in a straight line, \begin{equation} v = u + at \label{eq:v_uat} \end{equation} \begin{equation} s = ut + \tfrac12 at^2 \label{eq:s_uta} \end{equation} \begin{equation} s = \tfrac{1}{2}(u + v)t \label{eq:s_uvt} \end{equation} \begin{equation} v^2 = u^2 + 2as \label{eq:v_uas} \end{equation} \begin{equation} s = vt - \tfrac12 at^2 \label{eq:s_vat} \end{equation} Solve Eq. \ref{eq:s_uta} for $a$ to get \begin{equation} a=\frac{(s-ut)2}{t^2} \label{eq:a_sut} \end{equation} Substitute Eq. \ref{eq:a_sut} into Eq. \ref{eq:v_uat} to yield \begin{equation} v=u+\left(\frac{(s-ut)2}{t^2}\right) t \label{eq:v_ust_a_sub} \end{equation} Simplify Eq. \ref{eq:v_ust_a_sub} to get \begin{equation} v=u+\frac{(s-ut)2}{t} \label{eq:v_ust_t} \end{equation} Simplify Eq. \ref{eq:v_ust_t} to get \begin{equation} v=u+\frac{2s}{t}-2u \label{eq:v_ust_u} \end{equation} Simplify Eq. \ref{eq:v_ust_u} to get \begin{equation} v=\frac{2s}{t}-u \label{eq:v_ust} \end{equation}
